# Health Insurance Policy Document

## Section 1: Coverage Details

### 1.1 Medical Procedures Covered
This policy covers the following medical procedures when performed by qualified medical practitioners:

**Surgical Procedures:**
- Knee surgery including arthroscopy, meniscus repair, and knee replacement
- Hip replacement surgery for patients above 40 years of age
- Cataract surgery for all covered members
- Appendectomy and emergency surgical procedures
- Gallbladder surgery (cholecystectomy)
- Hernia repair surgery
- Bypass surgery for cardiac conditions
- Angioplasty and cardiac interventions

**Diagnostic Procedures:**
- MRI scans when medically necessary
- CT scans for diagnostic purposes
- Endoscopy procedures
- Biopsy procedures

### 1.2 Coverage Amounts
The following maximum coverage amounts apply per policy year:

- Knee surgery: Up to ₹2,00,000
- Hip replacement: Up to ₹3,00,000  
- Cataract surgery: Up to ₹50,000 per eye
- Cardiac procedures: Up to ₹5,00,000
- General surgery: Up to ₹1,50,000
- Emergency procedures: Up to ₹2,00,000

### 1.3 Geographic Coverage
This policy provides coverage for medical treatment received in the following cities and their surrounding areas:

**Tier 1 Cities:** Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune, Kolkata, Ahmedabad
**Tier 2 Cities:** Jaipur, Lucknow, Kanpur, Nagpur, Indore, Surat, Bhopal, Visakhapatnam
**Tier 3 Cities:** All other cities and towns in India

Treatment received in Tier 1 cities may have higher coverage limits compared to other tiers.

## Section 2: Waiting Periods

### 2.1 Initial Waiting Period
A waiting period of 30 days applies to all non-emergency treatments from the policy start date.

### 2.2 Pre-existing Conditions
Pre-existing medical conditions are covered after a waiting period of 24 months from the policy commencement date.

### 2.3 Specific Waiting Periods
- Maternity benefits: 9 months waiting period
- Knee and joint surgeries: 12 months waiting period for non-emergency cases
- Cardiac procedures: 24 months waiting period unless emergency
- Dental procedures: 6 months waiting period

## Section 3: Exclusions

### 3.1 General Exclusions
The following are not covered under this policy:

- Cosmetic surgery and aesthetic procedures
- Experimental or unproven treatments
- Treatment received outside India
- Self-inflicted injuries
- Injuries due to participation in hazardous sports
- Treatment for substance abuse

### 3.2 Age-Related Exclusions
- Patients above 70 years: Cardiac surgery exclusions apply
- Patients below 18 years: Certain adult-specific procedures excluded

## Section 4: Claim Procedures

### 4.1 Cashless Claims
For cashless treatment, the following process must be followed:
1. Inform the insurance company at least 48 hours before planned surgery
2. Obtain pre-authorization from the insurance company
3. Treatment must be received at a network hospital
4. Submit all required documents within 7 days of discharge

### 4.2 Reimbursement Claims
For reimbursement claims:
1. Submit claim form within 30 days of discharge
2. Provide original bills and medical reports
3. Include treating doctor's certification
4. Claims will be processed within 15 working days

## Section 5: Premium and Policy Terms

### 5.1 Premium Payment
Annual premium must be paid before the policy renewal date. Grace period of 15 days is provided for premium payment.

### 5.2 Policy Validity
This policy is valid for 12 months from the commencement date. Continuous renewal is available subject to terms and conditions.

### 5.3 Sum Insured
The sum insured amount varies based on the plan selected:
- Basic Plan: ₹2,00,000
- Standard Plan: ₹5,00,000  
- Premium Plan: ₹10,00,000
- Family Plan: ₹15,00,000

## Section 6: Special Provisions

### 6.1 Senior Citizens
Special provisions apply for policy holders above 60 years of age:
- Higher premium rates apply
- Certain exclusions may be waived after 3 years of continuous coverage
- Annual health check-ups are mandatory

### 6.2 Network Hospitals
The insurance company has tie-ups with over 5000 hospitals across India. Treatment at network hospitals enables cashless facility.

### 6.3 Emergency Treatment
Emergency treatment is covered from day one of the policy, subject to the following conditions:
- Life-threatening conditions only
- Treatment must commence within 24 hours of the emergency
- Hospitalization must be for minimum 24 hours
